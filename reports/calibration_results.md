## Step 7 — S_eval (single look) + selection

_Pre-registered rule:_ Lowest S_eval ECE wins; Brier breaks ties (lower wins). Reject any method whose S_eval reliability curve is non-monotone (observed frequency dropping by more than 0.02 between adjacent bins as mean predicted probability increases). This rule was pre-registered as a module constant before any S_eval number was looked at.

| method | ECE | Brier | monotone |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| raw (before) | 0.3305 | 0.2039 | — |
| platt | 0.0272 | 0.0919 | yes |
| isotonic | 0.0264 | 0.0917 | yes |

**Winner: `isotonic`.** ECE 0.3305 → 0.0264, Brier 0.2039 → 0.0917. See `figures/reliability_before_after.png`.

## Step 8 — class_weight diagnostic (read-only)

An UNWEIGHTED LightGBM (LOCKED params, `class_weight=None`) on S_fit gives CV AUROC **0.6722** (ranking unchanged vs the balanced 0.676 — discrimination is invariant to the weighting) and a NATIVE S_eval ECE of **0.0076**, versus the balanced model's 0.334. Unweighted training lands natively much closer to the base rate, confirming that the balanced weighting — not the features — is what inflates probabilities; calibration (above) is the principled fix rather than dropping the weighting. No artifact written, no registry change for this diagnostic.
