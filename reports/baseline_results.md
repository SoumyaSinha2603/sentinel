# Baseline Results — UCI Diabetes-130

> Honest floor. Two simple baselines through the frozen split harness on the default cohort. Minimal preprocessing only (no feature engineering, no tuning). Generated by `sentinel.models.baseline`.

## Setup

- Train / test rows: 79,541 / 19,802
- Rows dropped for NaN in the 8 numeric features: 0
- Train positive rate (trivial prediction): 0.1144
- Features: `number_inpatient`, `number_emergency`, `number_outpatient`, `number_diagnoses`, `num_medications`, `num_lab_procedures`, `time_in_hospital`, `num_procedures`

## Held-out test metrics

| model | AUROC | AUPRC | Brier | ECE |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| trivial (constant) | 0.5000 | 0.1119 | 0.0994 | 0.0000 |
| logistic (8 numeric) | 0.6267 | 0.1937 | 0.2330 | 0.3663 |

Test-set prevalence: 0.1119 (2,216 / 19,802). The trivial AUPRC equals prevalence and its AUROC is 0.5 by construction — that is the floor.

## Logistic cross-validation (locked folds, training portion)

Across 5 grouped CV folds:

- AUROC: **0.6336 ± 0.0075**
- AUPRC: **0.1986 ± 0.0086**

## Interpretation

Logistic test AUROC 0.6267 sits in the expected ~0.60-0.66 band for a simple model — consistent with the clean leakage audit and the honest ~0.68 ceiling. This is the number future models must beat; AUPRC and calibration (ECE) matter more than AUROC at this ~11% prevalence.
